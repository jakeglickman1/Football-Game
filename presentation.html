Project Snapshot

Retrobowl Sandlot compresses football drama into a single offensive drive while still tracking multi-week progress. The design pillars pulled from the README focus on responsive controls, readable pre-snap info, and progression hooks that reward repeated play.

Mouse-Aimed Throwing Hold + release to arc passes anywhere on the field, blending power meter timing with precision aiming.
Route Visualizer SVG overlays fade in before the snap so reads are clear; this is powered by preset lane + depth data per receiver.
Assignment-Based AI Defenders mirror their assigned target, delivering believable coverage without heavy physics systems.
Franchise Loop An eight-week season funnels into Semifinal + Championship rounds with trophy tracking between runs.

Controls & Feel

On-Field Inputs

  • Arrow keys move the QB or ball carrier.
  • Hold Shift to sprint during scrambles or after the catch.
  • Mouse click + hold charges the throw; release fires toward the cursor with stored power.
  • R resets the season if you want a fresh run during the demo.

Presentation Hooks

  • Line of scrimmage + first-down markers stay visible for situational awareness.
  • Animated trenches simulate pocket pressure so timing matters.
  • Receiver control swaps automatically once a catch is secured, keeping the momentum upfield.

Team Pool & Difficulty

10 stylized opponents (Cyclones, Gladiators, Kodiaks, Volt, Phantoms, Outlaws, Lynx, Comets, Sharks, Guardians) each carry a subtle difficulty scalar (0.92–1.12) that modifies defender speed and CPU scoring odds.

Route & Timing Tunables

Every receiver preset defines lane percentages, break points, final depths, and a speed cap; the SVG preview stays on screen for 2 seconds (ROUTE_PREVIEW_DURATION) to support pre-snap reads.

Roster, Draft, and Progression

Roster Rules

Season mode enforces a 5–12 player roster window (ROSTER_MIN_SIZE / ROSTER_MAX_SIZE) and starts you with eight archetypes (QB, RB, WR1, WR2, WR3, TE, LB, CB) so every phase of play has coverage.

Attribute Ranges

Each position references tailored stat bands (e.g., WR1 speed 68–98, QB accuracy 58–96) to ensure upgrades feel meaningful without breaking balance.

Draft Economy

The franchise loop grants one pick per round for three rounds (BASE_DRAFT_PICKS), surfacing three prospects per round (DRAFT_BOARD_SLOTS_PER_ROUND). Sign, release, or trade actions are wired in the UI so you can narrate personnel moves.

CPU Drive Modeling

Sim drives assume a 75-yard field (CPU_DRIVE_DEFAULT_DISTANCE) with base touchdown probability of 42% and field-goal fallback at 30%, giving enough randomness for drama during the presentation.

Big Factors Impacting Each Game

  • Play Calling Simplicity – One curated play per drive emphasizes execution; defender assignments keep it fair.
  • Power Meter Discipline – Over-charging floats the ball; under-charging leads to swats. Talk through how you feather throws for seams vs. outs.
  • Field Position Messaging – LOS + sticks + down text update each snap, letting the audience track success without glancing at the scoreboard.
  • Dynamic Branding – Opponent labels and end-zone colors pull from the schedule generator so every matchup looks bespoke.
  • Live Route Adjustments – Receivers bend toward final lanes mid-play, so leading passes vs. waiting for breaks becomes a coaching point you can highlight.
